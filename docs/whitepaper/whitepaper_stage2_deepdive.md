# Stage 2: The Computational Layer (Deep Dive)

## 2.1 From Sharing Tools to Sharing "Brainpower"
In Stage 1, neighbors share physical objects. In Stage 2, they share the underutilized "brainpower" of their digital objects—specifically, the Neural Processing Units (NPUs) in their smartphones.

**Eco-Compute** is the act of contributing idle processing cycles to a community-beneficial computational network while the user sleeps.

## 2.2 The Community Treasury: Revenue & Incentives
KULA aggregates thousands of smartphones into a virtual supercomputer and sells this "Edge Compute" to third parties (e.g., AI startups, research institutions). 

**The 60/20/20 Revenue Model:**
*   **60% to the Contributor:** Credited as "KULA Credits" to the user’s account, which can be used to "pay" for premium community services or physical goods within the ecosystem.
*   **20% to Neighborhood Funds:** Channelled into a democratically managed treasury for local projects (e.g., repairing a park, funding a street festival, or a local universal basic income pilot).
*   **20% to Infrastructure:** To cover the costs of the central orchestration and ongoing platform development.

## 2.3 Hardware Safety: The "Nightstand Test"
To ensure participation is risk-free, KULA implements a hardware-aware safety protocol. Computation *only* begins when the following "Sensor Fusion" conditions are met:
1.  **Power State:** Device is connected to a power source.
2.  **Thermal Headroom:** Device internal temperature is below 35°C.
3.  **Stationary Test:** Accelerometer detects zero movement (not in a pocket or bag).
4.  **Environment Test:** Proximity and Ambient Light sensors confirm the device is unobstructed (ensuring it is not under a pillow or trapped in a heat-retaining fabric).

**The Thermal Governor:** If the device temperature rises towards 38°C, the app automatically scales down to a "Light Performance Profile." If it hits 40°C, all computation is aborted immediately.

## 2.4 The Market: Distributed AI Inference
Traditional AI training happens in energy-hungry data centers. KULA focuses on **AI Inference** (running the models). 
*   **Efficiency:** Running small, optimized LLMs on a phone's NPU is significantly more energy-efficient than running them on a giant data center GPU.
*   **Sustainability:** By using existing hardware, KULA avoids the carbon footprint of building new server farms and provides a "greener" compute option for the world.
