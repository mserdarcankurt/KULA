# KULA: Glossary of Terms

This glossary defines the core concepts and terminology used within the KULA project and whitepaper.

### Imece (Collective Labor)
A traditional Anatolian concept of mutual aid where members of a community contribute labor to help a neighbor. KULA digitalizes this as "Eco-Compute"—the collective contribution of smartphone processing power.

### KULA Mycelium
The visualization of the "Web of Trust." Like a fungal network in a forest, it reveals the invisible social threads and indirect bonds (Neighbor-of-Neighbor) that connect individuals within a community.

### The Nightstand Test
A hardware safety protocol. It ensures Eco-Compute only begins when a device is charging, stationary, cool (<35°C), and unobstructed (on a nightstand, not under a pillow).

### Closed-Loop Transaction
A socioeconomic narrative where every action requires an immediate, equivalent reaction or payment. KULA challenges this by fostering non-transactional collective existence.

### Community Treasury
A democratically managed fund fueled by revenue from collective computational work (Stage 2). It is used for local grants, repairs, and community-chosen projects.

### Trustee Request
An active form of social vouching. By becoming a "Trustee" for a neighbor, you take responsibility for their presence in the network, strengthening the overall Social Mycelium.

### Invitation Lineage
The "Social DNA" of a member. It tracks the chain of invitations back to the original community nodes, ensuring accountability and high trust.

### Socioeconomic Mesh
The final stage of KULA where social trust and physical mesh networking (Stage 3) merge to create an infrastructure that is independent of centralized internet or financial systems.

### Thermal Governor
A software-level safety mechanism that monitors device temperature and throttles or halts computation to protect battery health and device longevity.

### Trust-Weighted Voting
A governance model where influence over the Community Treasury is determined by a user's depth and history within the Social Mycelium, rather than financial stake.
